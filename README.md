# 🚦 Smart Traffic MARL  
**Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Adaptive Traffic Optimization in Smart Cities**

---

## 🌍 Vision

Urban mobility is evolving into one of the most complex challenges in modern infrastructure systems. Rapid urbanization, increasing vehicle ownership, and dynamic commuting patterns are pushing traditional traffic systems beyond their limits.

This research explores how **decentralized, intelligent multi-agent systems** can transform traffic networks into **adaptive, self-optimizing ecosystems**, capable of responding to real-world conditions in real time.

---

## 🎯 Mission

To design and evaluate a **real-time, AI-driven traffic signal control framework** that:

- Minimizes congestion and idle time  
- Improves traffic throughput across intersections  
- Enables decentralized decision-making  
- Scales across smart cities and evolving infrastructure  

---

## 🇦🇪 Context: Traffic Challenges in the UAE

The United Arab Emirates, particularly Dubai and Abu Dhabi, represents one of the most advanced smart city ecosystems globally. However, **traffic congestion remains a persistent and growing challenge**.

### 📊 Observations from Real Data

- Dubai recorded **~42.7% congestion levels in 2025**, among the highest in the region  
- A **10 km trip takes ~19+ minutes on average**, increasing during peak hours  
- Abu Dhabi shows **~29.6% congestion**, with increasing trends year-over-year  
- **86% of UAE residents report experiencing congestion**, with most indicating it is worsening  
- Peak-hour gridlocks are consistently reported across major corridors such as E311 and E611

## 🚦 Current Traffic Management Systems

Modern traffic systems in the UAE rely on a mix of infrastructure and semi-adaptive technologies:

### 1. Fixed-Time Signal Control  
- Predefined signal cycles  
- No real-time adaptability  

### 2. Sensor-Based Adaptive Systems  
- Use cameras and loop detectors  
- Limited responsiveness  
- Centralized decision-making  

### 3. Infrastructure-Led Optimization  
- Road expansions and flyovers  
- Metro and rail systems  
- Smart mobility initiatives  

---

## ⚠️ Limitations of Existing Mechanisms

Despite significant investments, key inefficiencies persist:

- ❌ Reactive rather than predictive behavior  
- ❌ Limited coordination between intersections  
- ❌ Centralized control bottlenecks  
- ❌ Poor handling of dynamic traffic surges  
- ❌ Increased idle time → fuel wastage and emissions  

**Key Insight:**  
Increasing road capacity alone does not sustainably reduce congestion and can lead to **induced demand**, worsening long-term traffic conditions.

## 🧠 Proposed Research

This research proposes a **Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL)** framework for traffic optimization.

### Core Concept

- Each intersection acts as an **independent intelligent agent**  
- Agents learn optimal policies through **real-time environment interaction**  
- Coordination emerges **implicitly through shared traffic dynamics**  

### System Design

- Grid-based simulation (e.g., 4×4 intersections)  
- Comparative evaluation:
  - Baseline (fixed-time system)  
  - MARL-based adaptive system  

### Evaluation Metrics

- Average waiting time  
- Queue length  
- Throughput  
- Congestion index
